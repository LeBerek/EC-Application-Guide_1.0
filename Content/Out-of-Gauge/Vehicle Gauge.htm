Vehicle Gauge

Vehicle gauge is one of the technical characteristics of a railway vehicle and concerns the permitted size of a vehicle. More precisely, the vehicle gauge is a set of rules encompassing a reference profile and its associated calculation rules, which determine the vehicle’s maximum exterior dimensions and the required clearance area within the infrastructure to prevent contact by the vehicle with the structure gauge.

Rolling stock constructors apply one of the three calculation methods. A vehicle's conformity with a reference profile is declared based on a calculation method referred to as:

  • Static.
  • Kinematic.
  • Dynamic.

The calculation methods within a single reference profile, for example G1, are independent. That is, depending on the calculation method used, the vehicle design will make more or less use of the railway lines' room capacity provided by the infrastructure manager.

The static method is the oldest calculation method, and the most conservative. At the same time, it is the simplest to apply. Also, the method that RUs apply for the evaluation of consignment dimensions, so they can determine whether they constitute an exceptional consignment, is based on this method.

Since 2009, wagons have been marked with the kinematic gauge reference profiles to which they were built. Since 2014, the gauge marking has been updated and additionally includes a separate marking of the lower and upper vehicle gauges.

Vehicle gauges are indicated by letters:

  • G with the numeral 1 or 2 for regular rolling stock gauges - example: G1
  • G with the letter A, B or C for newer widened in the upper part gauges - example: GC
  • G with additional letter I and with the numeral 1, 2 or 3 for the lower than 400 mm from the rail head part of the vehicle - example: GI2

Only the vehicle gauge G1/GI1 is a truly international vehicle gauge (except for Great Britain). Other vehicle gauges might require additional checks.

Before operating authorised vehicles, the RU should conduct a Route compatibility check in line with its Safety Management System SMS. Interoperable, Multinational and National gauges do not currently contain data. These parameters are left for informational reasons only.

Vehicle markings G1, G2 on wagons and other vehicles refer to the kinematic gauges as specified in TSIs.

Some IMs, for example in Poland, require all vehicles brought onto their network by an RU to conform with the static vehicle gauge profiles.

For IMs requiring conformity with the static vehicle gauge, just comparing the ERATV data and RINF data may be insufficient to decide whether a vehicle built to kinematic gauge actually conforms with the static gauge provisions and is compatible with the static vehicle gauge profile. In such a case the only way to examine the vehicle's size is to run an inspection of a detailed vehicle drawing or a study with calculations of the vehicle's restriction analysis. The later type of document is rarely made available to RUs.
