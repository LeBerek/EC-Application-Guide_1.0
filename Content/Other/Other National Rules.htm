Other – National Rules

Basic provisions concerning exceptional consignments agreed by all UIC members are set in UIC IRS 50502. The IRS document also stipulates that, in some countries, the networks of certain managers are governed by rules different from those set out in the IRS. Note, not all deviations from the rules are mentioned in this document.

In addition to the provisions described in the IRS, it is necessary to familiarise yourself in detail with the technical and operational rules of the network in question and the network manager's instructions for exceptional consignments before preparing an application for an exceptional transport authorisation.

Train Length - Special Case

Maximum train length as such is not a reason for exceptional consignment train. As a rule, the train length should in all cases be shorter than the effective length of the tracks present at departure, overtaking and arrival for which the train is planned.

While agreeing the conditions for exceptional transport, especially in international traffic, keep in mind that route-specific length restrictions may also apply on:

  • First mile (service infrastructure, terminal and port infrastructure)
  • Border routes (specified in border agreements with neighbour infrastructure)
  • Last mile (service infrastructure, terminal and port infrastructure)

When planning the route, take into account the shortest allowed train length on the entire planned route. Perhaps the train has to be split somewhere (e.g. for loading or unloading the train set because of loading ramp length). If so, you may need to request a route for exceptional consignment which enables train split, and is not treated as an additional permit just for the train split.
