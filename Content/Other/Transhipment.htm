Transhipment to different track gauges

Definition

Load units to be transhipped if they weigh more than 25 tonnes or are loaded on low-loader wagons (only applies to transhipment to railways with different track gauges).

Reasons for exceptional transport conditions

This rule for exceptional consignments applies when a shipment is transferred between broader gauges, tracks such as:

  • Iberian Gauge: 1668 mm (Spain and Portugal)
  • Russian Gauge: 1520 mm (countries of the former Soviet Union) inclusive of Estonia (old tracks), Latvia, Lithuania, Moldova and Ukraine

and standard gauge 1435 mm (the majority of continental Europe).

Three reasons why the transhipment of goods heavier than 25 tonnes is exceptional:

  1. Vehicle characteristics – standard gauge wagons have different geometrical characteristic than broad gauge wagons. Therefore, they cannot simply change the tractive unit and continue on the standard tracks. Broad gauge (1520 mm) wagons use different coupling and braking systems than standard gauge wagons. In order for broad gauge wagons to enter standard gauge track, they would have to have their wheelset spacing adjusted to 1435 mm. In most cases broad gauge wagons do not have systems for an automatic wheelset spacing change. In addition, they are too big to enter the standard gauge network. Load transhipment onto standard track wagons is therefore the preferred option.
  2. Loading gauge restriction – goods loaded on broad gauge wagons might be a normal consignment on broad gauge rail network. After transhipment onto standard wagons and while on a standard track rail network they may become out of gauge loads. For example, loaded ISO High Cube containers while transported on standard gauge wagons they become out of gauge exceptional consignments.
  3. Complexity of transhipment – arising from two main factors:
    • The diverse range of load types weighing over 25 tonnes, combined with various transhipment technologies.
    • The varying quality of transhipment facilities and differences in transport regulations contribute to the overall complexity of the operation of transhipment from wagon to wagon.
