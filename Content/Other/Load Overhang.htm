Load Overhang

Rigid goods which overhang over the coupling zone may swing out further than the wagon body when the wagon runs on curved tracks. This is why it is necessary to reduce the width of the load overhang to take the load's protrusion into account when examining a consignment’s conformity with the requested route.

Load's overhang, as such, is not a reason for treatment as exceptional consignment.
You deal with a rigid long consignment when the load:

  • Is loaded on one wagon.
  • Protrudes over the:
    • Maximum loading length.
    • Coupling zone.
  • Does not exceed the length of 10 m, when it's overhang is measured longitudinally from the bogie pivot axis to the front or the end edge of consignment.
  • Is transported with protection wagon.

In addition, in such cases always check:

  • That the load width restrictions which are set in UIC Loading Guidelines Tables are observed.
  • The larger value of the overhang, when the load overhangs at both ends of the wagon.
  • The axle load values especially when the load's centre of gravity is displaced in relation to the central intersection of the three axes of symmetry of the wagon under consideration.

It is essential to carry out the above checks since these loads, due to their dimensions of length and width and because of their shifted centre of gravity, may become reasons for exceptional consignments, as out-of-gauge or heavy transports.
