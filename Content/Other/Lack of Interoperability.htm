Lack of Interoperability

European Vehicles running on their own wheels when they do not have the required interoperability in a country forming part of the route or approval/authorisation to place in service become exceptional consignments. For such vehicles, each application submitted for an exceptional consignment should contain a note on the missing vehicle's authorisation in the countries concerned.

Missing authorisation

Applies equally to vehicles which are empty, loaded, or which themselves are the subject of a contract of carriage, and when they are not registered as authorised (placed to service) in the country concerned, or which do not have the inscriptions or respective marks:

  • Passenger coaches
    • RIC
  • Freight wagons
    • TEN GE
    • RIV
    • Acceptance marking: TEN + G1 or concerned country acronym in approval plate
  • Traction units and multiple units
    • Concerned country acronym in approval plate
  • Special vehicles
    • Concerned country acronym in approval plate or IM's/EVU s code in bi- or multi- lateral agreement grid

EVN Number

In the EU , railway vehicles are registered in the European Vehicle Register (EVR).
Registered vehicles have a 12-digit EVN number which contains information on the vehicles technical characteristics. Checking the first digit of the EVN number in combination with the country where the vehicle is registered allows you to decide whether you are dealing with an authorised or a missing authorisation vehicle.

Additional Requirements

In the absence of a vehicle's authorisation, the transport of vehicles on their own wheels requires supplementary technical and operational details. This information, necessary for the application to IM, can be sourced from the vehicle’s documentation or, to some extent, from its markings. Compiled data should be incorporated into the authorisation application by the dispatching RU or added as an appendix.

For vehicles towed on their own wheels without their own power fewer declarations are required in addition to the application. For vehicles running with their own power, IMs require more detailed declarations on compliance with the planned route.

Vehicles without authorisation to place in service or without approval.

Two tools can be attempted to retrieve vehicle information: for vehicles – ERATV, and for routes - RINF. If no information is available for the vehicle in question, ask the client to provide it. State the corresponding declarations in the application, under items #28 on vehicle properties and #29 on vehicle operating properties.

For the initial check you need to examine:

  • Member state of authorisation.
  • Current authorisation status.
  • Certificate for the ability to run.
  • Vehicle's gauge - reference profile.
  • Continuous brake pipe availability and efficiency.
  • Type of traction (optional).
  • Buffing and coupling gear.
  • Distance between two adjacent axles not exceeding 17 500 mm.
  • Distance between the last axle and the buffers.
  • Smallest negotiable track radius.
  • Minimum in service wheel diameter.
  • The ability to be hump shunted.
  • Position where to put into the train (any, the last or as the rear of the train).

Additional questions and declarations may become necessary depending on the type of vehicle and on the operational compatibility of the vehicle.

A new vehicle in testing phase may already have a 12-digit EVN assigned. Ask the customer about:

  • EVN assigned to the vehicle.
  • Manufacturer's vehicle number, when EVN is not yet assigned.

In code #2a you can declare vehicle numbers individually or as a vehicle group list, when more vehicles are transported at once.

When you deal with a newly built freight wagon with EVN with the first digit 2 or 3, then be cautious when assessing the status of wagon's authorisation for the network. Newly built wagons may have the EVN with "reserved" status. In code #2b inform the IM respectively. See possible statuses of EVN.
