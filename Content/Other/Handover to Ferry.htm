Handover to Ferry

Most goods shipped by rail to ports are routed to harbour loading quays or port terminals, depending on their type and the transfer method to the ship. Some European ports accept also train-ferry service. In both cases of exceptional consignment trains going to a port, it is necessary to:

  • Ascertain to which specific tracks in the port area the train with the exceptional consignment is to be directed.
  • Inquire whether exceptional consignment approval is also granted by the port infrastructure managers for this type of consignment.

Definition

When consignments that are to be transshipped to a ferry, do not meet the regulations of General Contract of Use of wagons (GCU), Appendix 14, Part A, they constitute another type of exceptional consignment.

Use Cases

Rail-ferry transfer locations and specific rules are listed in Part A of GCU Annex 14. Specific ports and ferries have their individual characteristics of train access and therefore certain restrictions that wagons must fulfil to be accepted onto a ferry. Observe wagon marking:

In these cases of wagon transfer to ferries, the restrictions also include the necessity off obtaining an exceptional consignment authorisation:

  • Wagons with two- or three-axle bogies that do not meet the GCU Appendix 14 Part A
  • Wagons with bogies that have more than three axles
  • Shipments carried on more than one wagon
  • Goods carried on two wagons coupled together
  • Loads carried with a buffer wagon
